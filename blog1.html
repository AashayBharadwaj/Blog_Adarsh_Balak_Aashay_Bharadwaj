The evolution of Currency

Before we directly jump onto
Bitcoin, it's important for us to understand the problems posed by the conventional system.

To understand the origin and evolution of currencies we need to look back at least 3000 years in history. Humans used to exchange goods for goods that we call the barter system. Around 1200 BC currencies used for trading were animal skins, weapons and agricultural products. This changed in 1100 BC when chinese used the small replica casts of the weapons as the exchange goods. These casts were replicated on circular shaped bronze metals which are considered the beginning of coinage. It was in the 6th century BC that Lydia’s king created the first official currency. The coins were stamped with pictures that appeared to be the denominations. Lydia used gold coins for trade and the standard means of exchange, now the exchange of goods turned into purchase of goods. While Lydia was dominating in the use of currency, China started the use of paper money instead of coins. Europe was using coins till1600 AD, after which the banks started using notes which could be exchanged for gold or silver coins. The notes were used by banks and personal institutions and not by the government. The first paper note was issued by the colonial government in North America.. The use of paper currency increased international trades and banks started buying currencies from different countries that formed the first currency market.

Our journey so far:-

Types of currencies:
Digital currency:
It is a virtual currency created by a pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto that has no physical coinage.
Coins and Banknotes:
The money that is in circulation or used to exchange can be referred to as currency, especially circulating banknotes and coins.
E,g American dollars, British pounds, European euros.
Narrow money:
The money that is in physical form like the notes, coins and other forms of money that can be converted to cash.
Broad money :
It includes physical money and also more than that like deposits in the bank, saving accounts and non institutionalised money market.
Gold:
A rare form of economy that is rare and has high value in the market.
The value of gold depends on the rarity, industrial process and investments.

Why is there so much hype around blockchain technology? There have been many attempts to create digital money in the past, but they have always failed. The prevailing issue is trust. If someone creates a new currency called the X dollar, how can we trust that they won't give themselves a million X dollars, or steal your X dollars for themselves? Bitcoin was designed to solve this problem by using a specific type of database called a blockchain. Most normal databases, such as an SQL database, have someone in charge who can change the entries (e.g. giving themselves a million X dollars). Blockchain is different because nobody is in charge; it’s run by the people who use it. What’s more, bitcoins can’t be faked, hacked or double spent – so people that own this money can trust that it has some value.
